February
Implement user authentication and design the staff management and user profile settings screens. Continue working on the web based clinic letter editor.

March
Finish the initial design for the letter editor and release a promotional video showing some of the key concepts of the system.

April
Document and develop the API’s for syncing patient demographics, employee and clinic lists data with the main hospital systems. Publish the documentation. Incorporate the clinic list work flow into the system.

May
Integrate the address book with the Royal Mail PAF database to ensure proper addressing of external recipients. Add CC management to the letter editor. Integrate the medical spell checker with the letter editor.

June
Set-up & configure LaTeX to typeset the letters based on the number of recipients. Internal recipients & GP’s will receive their letters electronically after being signed. Copies for patients and other external recipients can be batched printed using the PDF’s produced by LaTeX.

July
Develop the work flow allowing consultants to digitally sign the finished PDF.

August
Stress test the database design using 10 million letters. Stress test a single patient having 50,000 letters. General performance tuning.

September
Implement work flows for teams such as waiting list, 18 week & clinical coding.

October
Start developing audit and management reports to facilitate performance monitoring.

Into 2016

  • Integration of scanned content into the patients file
  • Remote validation of letters for consultants
  • Patient access to their clinic letters via a secure two factor authenticated login.
  • Integration of digital dictation
